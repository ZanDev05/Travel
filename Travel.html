Moalboal White Beach (Basdaku)

Basdaku, which literally means "big sand" in Bisaya, is one of Cebu's longest coastlines. Basdaku in Moalboal is approximately one kilometer long and has exquisite white sands and transparent waters that rival Boracay! You'll never be bored here because there are so many activities to choose from. This South Cebu beach is a must-visit for sunbathing and stargazing. Visitors can experience Moalboal's widest and prettiest coastline for a low entrance fee of P10 to P20.

Panagsama Beach

Do you want to go on a relaxing island vacation? Join the locals at Panagsama Beach, a 16-minute ride from Basdaku, Moalboal. Panagsama, also known as Basdiot, is the beach for beach lovers seeking a respite from Cebu's hectic tourist hordes. At first sight, Panagsama may appear to be your normal neighborhood beach, but many people are unaware of its attractions, which include a vibrant underwater scene. The beach, which is lined with beach resorts and diving schools, is Moalboal's best snorkeling location. Because it is a jumping off place for, you can meet a variety of sea creatures and have a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to travel on, where you will be surrounded by massive schools of swimming sardines in Cebu's cleanest seas. Also, keep a watch out for jumping dolphins! Entrance to Panagsama Beach is free.

Marine Life in Pescador Island

Moalboal, Philippines, is a popular tourist destination in Visayas due to its reef diving locations, one of which is located on Pescador Island. The reefs on the tiny island slowly slope downward; soft coral covers the first 5 to 10 meters offshore before dropping abruptly to 40 meters, forming a hard reef wall. Pescador Island's reefs support hundreds, if not thousands, of different fish species. This is why adventure trips to Pescador Island are popular among both locals and tourists.

Moalboal Sardine Run

If you've ever wanted to swim with a big school of fish, Moalboal will make your dreams come true with its thick concentration of sardines swimming in mass along a reef wall 30 meters off Panagsama Beach. The Moalboal Sardine Run tour remains mostly in the Panagsama Beach area throughout the year, allowing you to visit this remarkable natural occurrence at any time. If you're lucky, you might even spot a thresher shark looking for sardines to eat. For a truly immersive experience, try this one-of-a-kind adventure in Moalboal.

Dive at Moalboal House Reef

A dive spot in Moalboal does not require a long boat ride. Moalboal's House Reef is only a few meters offshore, so you can walk from the beach right into the dive! Don't worry about the quality of marine life you can see from a reef so near to shore! Even from this distance, you'll notice plenty of coral, beautiful sea sponges, fish, octopuses, and other marine life.

Go Snorkeling at Tuble Marine Sanctuary

After exploring the Moalboal House Reef, drive to White Beach to visit the Tuble Marine Sanctuary. Its relatively shallow waters make it an ideal snorkeling spot. Even at 10 meters deep, you may witness a diverse range of marine life, including Hawksbill and Green Turtles.

Best Time to Visit Moalboal

Beach season never ends, especially on the sunny island of Cebu! However, to make the most of your holiday, avoid the rainy season, when the waters become too murky for a pleasant swim. If you want to see the best South Cebu beaches, plan your vacation for the summer. The greatest time to visit this coastal refuge is from January to early June, when you may freely swim, snorkel, and witness the sardines, coral reefs, and sea turtles in all their magnificence.
